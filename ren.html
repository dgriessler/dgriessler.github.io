Balanced MSE for Imbalanced Visual Regression

Summary

In this paper, they introduce Balanced Mean Squared Error (Balanced MSE) over the widely used Mean Squared Error (MSE) for imbalanced regression. MSE is found to perform worse on the minority class because of the imbalanced dataset. They show through some math that regressors trained with MSE underestimate on the minority class.

Balanced MSE has two parts: the first is standard MSE and the second is a balancing term. Calculating this term can be difficult. They offer one way through expressing p_train(y) as a Gaussian Mixture Model (GMM). This constrains p_train to be a Gaussian model. They also offer an approach by using Monte Carlo Method to approximate p_train(y). A form called Batch-based Monte-Carlo (BMC) doesn't need any prior knowledge about p_train(y).
