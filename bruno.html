Empirical Model of 10 – 130 MeV Solar Energetic Particle Spectra at 1 AU Based on Coronal Mass Ejection Speed and Direction

Summary

Presents a model for predicting SEP event-integrated and peak intensity spectra between 10 and 130 MeV at 1 Au based on STEREO, GOES, and PAMELA satellite experiement. SEP intensities are reconstrcted from 32 SEP events between 2010 and 2014 using a 2D Gaussian functional form which is energy dependent and accounting for the correctional between the intensity and speed of the CME and magnetic field-line connection angle. The CME measurements are from DONKI. The model is tested using 20 SEP events not used to derive the formula. The observed and predicted event-integrated and peak intensities at Earth and at the STEREO spacecraft agree in spectral shape and absolute value.

The formula referenced is the same one as mentioned in Prediction of Solar Energetic Particle Event Peak Proton Intensity Using a Simple Algorithm Based on CME Speed and Direction and Observations of Associated Solar Phenomena. There is some intensive math in the section for SEP Intensity Spatial Distribution. Results are produced for peak and event-integrated intensity as a function of longitudinal connection angle. These form Gaussian curves with a center often around 43 degrees. CME speed is shown to have a linear correlation with the intensity values between 10 and 130 MeV in both event integrated and peak. Their formula allows them to plot a band of prediction for 10-130 MeV instead of just a singular value as predicted in 14-24 MeV peak intensities by the referenced formula.
